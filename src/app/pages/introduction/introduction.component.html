Hey, I'm Mat, and welcome to RetroSki!
I've always been fascinated by games that are easy to learn but hard to master.
That's exactly what RetroSki is all about: a simple yet skill-based retro 2D skiing game that lets you enjoy the ride while constantly improving.
The slopes are randomly generated, designed to match one of four alpine skiing disciplines, so every run is different.
Whether you're practicing your slalom turns or flying down the downhill course, there's always something new to explore.
Back in January 2024, I was looking for a game to play with my girlfriend. Something simple, fun, and fair, where we could both start on equal footing.
Turns out, that wasn't so easy to find. So, I decided to make one myself.
Maybe not my best idea... Developing the game gave me a bit of a head start. But don't be fooled, Emi has become a fierce competitor. Watch out!
From the start, I wanted RetroSki to be a game you could enjoy without interruptions. No pop-ups, no forced ads, just pure gameplay.
At the same time, I also wanted the game to be fully accessible for free, so anyone could pick it up and have fun.
To keep things fair, RetroSki offers optional premium memberships, which lets you create your own private servers to race with friends.
But whether you're a free rider or a premium one, the core experience stays the same: just you, the slopes, and the thrill of the ride.
RetroSki is an open-source project.
The best way to support the game? Enjoy it! Have fun, challenge yourself, and share it with your friends.
If you'd like to go further, you can also support RetroSki by subscribing to a Premium membership to host online event, or making a small donation to help keep the project alive.
However you choose to ride, I appreciate every rider who joins the slopes. Thank you!
RetroSki collects only the bare minimum needed to run the game smoothly.
No tracking, no selling of data, no ads following you around. Your information stays private and is never used for commercial purposes.
